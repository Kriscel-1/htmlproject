Quarter 2- Module 3: Solving Problems Involving Linear Inequalities in Two Variables

This module delves into solving problems involving linear inequalities in two variables. Students will learn various strategies to solve and interpret solutions to real-world problems modeled by linear inequalities.

Key topics covered in this module include:

  • Identifying constraints and objectives in linear inequality problems
  • Using graphical and algebraic methods to find feasible regions
  • Optimizing solutions to maximize or minimize objectives
  • Interpreting solutions in the context of the problem

By mastering the concepts presented in this module, students will develop problem-solving skills essential for analyzing and solving real-world problems involving linear inequalities in two variables.
